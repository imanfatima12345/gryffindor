Gryffindor House was founded by Godric Gryffindor, one of the four founders of Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry in the 10th century. Known for his courage, boldness, and sense of justice, Godric valued bravery, daring, and chivalry above all else. He believed that witches and wizards should be chosen for their character rather than just their magical ability. The house is symbolized by the lion, representing strength and courage, and its colors are scarlet and gold. Its common room lies in Gryffindor Tower, guarded by the portrait of the Fat Lady. Over the centuries, Gryffindor House became home to some of the most famous witches and wizards in history, including Albus Dumbledore, Harry Potter, Hermione Granger, and Ron Weasley.
